Blockchain and Cryptocurrencies

What does it do?

Cryptocurrency is a new form of modern currency that exclusively exist on a digital or virtual platform. Unlike regular currencies, cryptocurrencies do not have a central regulating authority (a single system that monitors and manages all different transactions). Instead, cryptocurrencies will use a decentralised system to record new transactions and issue new units.  What separates cryptocurrency to conventional money is the tangibility of the currency as well as the method of security. As cryptocurrency isn’t tangible, individuals who own cryptocurrency own a “key” to a “wallet” which is how you access the cryptocurrency you own. On a security basis, cryptocurrencies use a method of “encryption” to verify transactions. The aim of this encryption is to provide security and safety.  The encryption used in cryptocurrency varies between different cryptocurrency. For example, the most well-known cryptocurrency: Bitcoin uses a mathematical technique used for a type of encryption based on elliptic curves; also known as the Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm (ECDSA). The main advocacy for cryptocurrency stems from this security. It is estimated that current computers are unable to break the encryption in Bitcoin. This emphasis on safety and security is why there is a cultural shift for money to be acquired virtually.  There are several different cryptocurrencies that exist in today’s market. The most recent and well-known one are: Bitcoin, Ethereum, XRP, Polkadot and Dogecoin. Cryptocurrency like any other currency can also be traded. As a result, huge sums of money exist in the cryptocurrency market. As cryptocurrency is quite novel in current technology and economy, it is known as quite volatile in the stock market. This means trading with cryptocurrency can lead to massive growths and losses in revenue for traders. It is important that this currency should be further developed to ensure a more stable trading market as well as improving economy in society.  Cryptocurrency is tightly linked to a concept and technology called blockchain. In simplistic terms, blockchain is a system of recording information that makes it incredibly difficult to change or hack the system. The hype around the blockchain technology stems from the fact that it solves one of the prevailing issues surrounding digital currency: the issue of trust. As blockchain is a database where the users run the database instead of a normal database such as an SQL database where there is  s a centra authority that monitors the system, it means that there is a consensus of mutual trust amongst the users in the blockchain network.  Talking about blockchain in technical terms, the decentralised database that is blockchain is also known  as Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT). Each participant of a blockchain has a ledger (block). When a new transaction occurs in the blockchain, it is recorded to every participant’s ledger. Cryptocurrency which uses this blockchain technology records the transactions with an immutable cryptocurrency signature, also known as a “hash”. The implication of immutability is that if a single “block” or ledger is altered or changed for malicious intent, it would immediately become noticeable that there was tampering of the system.  A common comparison with blockchain is the conventional bank. There are many who have presumed that blockchains may replace brick and mortar banks in the future but there are many differences. While there are apparent benefits with a blockchain, such as a faster transaction speed, ease of transfers as well as a complete sense of anonymity, there are other concerns such as a difficulty of government tracking (not knowing who runs what account means it may be difficult to seize it). This type of cultural view may shift in the future.

What is the likely impact?

If blockchain technology is implemented into the financial banking sector, payment security, transparency and efficiency can improve as blockchain is a shared ledger technology where everyone can see the status and history of transactions which can help clients and firms work together more efficiently. This technology has potential to replace centralized banking with a decentralized system but with the uncertainty and big banks and government not in favor of this technology, this is unlikely to happen. The use of blockchain technology can also improve cyber security as the blockchain network is resistant to hacks and unauthorized changes due to being encrypted with advanced cryptography. The blockchain network also allows individuals to directly perform transactions with one another through a single distributed ledger instead of relying on a third party which would be more susceptible to vulnerabilities than the blockchain network. Crypto currencies can be used for illegal activities as they some anonymity but if the individual has linked an email or is using an exchange or wallet then the wallet would be traceable to the individual. Crypto currencies can be used in day-to-day life and may even replace countries' currencies but due to the high volatility, make it almost impossible to happen as it is not backed by anything.

How will this affect you?

Block chain technology can positively affect us as it would increase security when performing transactions of any sort as well as increasing the efficacy and speed of said transactions and processes. Crypto currency can positively affect us as it allows us to use another form of currency if the firm/business allows it. Additionally crypto currency can be used as another form of investment with many cryptocurrencies increasing in value over time such as bitcoin and Ethereum which we can use to enrich our lively hood.

Autonomous Vehicles

What does it do?

An autonomous vehicle is a driverless vehicle that is capable of sensing its surroundings and can operate itself and perform necessary functions safely with little or no human input. This is done through the use of high-resolution cameras, light detection and ranging which allows the vehicles to figure out their surroundings. The current state of the art autonomous vehicles would be Tesla Autopilot and GM Super Cruise. Additionally, Mercedes-Benz was the world's first automaker to 'gain internationally valid regulatory approval in producing vehicles capable of Level 3 autonomous or "conditionally automated" driving' (Alvin 2022, para. 1). Currently, by looking at the state of autonomous vehicles, we have already reached impressive milestones as companies such as Aptiv, Yandex and Waymo offer autonomous taxis in dedicated areas. Furthermore, big brands such as tesla have developed Tesla Autopilot, which is a suite of advanced driver-assistance system that amounts to Level 2 vehicle automation. Tesla themselves state that 'Tesla cars come standard with advanced hardware capable of providing Autopilot features, and full self-driving capabilities—through software updates designed to improve functionality over time' (Tesla 2022). Within the upcoming years, Tesla is expected to achieve Level 4 autonomy. Level 4 is considered to be fully autonomous driving where the vehicle can handle the majority of driving situations by itself. Although a human can still request control of the vehicle while in this state.

What is the likely impact?

Autonomous vehicles will have a significant impact on society for a number of reasons, some positives include the number of vehicle accidents could be reduced and travel time can be used more effectively due to reduced traffic jams. As we continue to further develop and advance our current technologies, autonomous vehicles will likely reach a state where they will be able to have the full capabilities to drive all on their own without any type of human interaction needed with maximum safety levels. If we do reach this state, roads would overall be safer due to all vehicles being automated designed to drive safely. This will cause fewer accidents caused by human error on the roads. However, this would also impact those working in transport related sectors like car repair centers due to less accidents occurring. Overall, if this advancement comes, it should be expected to make many jobs redundant. Namely jobs such as uber would easily be replaced by autonomous vehicles causing many individuals who drive for uber to lose their jobs. However, there could also be the opportunity to open new jobs regarding the development of these autonomous vehicles. The advancement of autonomous vehicles may cause the demand for human drivers in the transportation sector to disappear.

Project Idea

The global COVID-19 pandemic has put surface disinfectants to the test. It has also prompt organizations to develop ultraviolet-disinfection or otherwise known as UV-C robots. These robots are becoming more popular as an option for disinfecting rooms and spaces of all surfaces in one process, and as a result, they are appealing to hospital administrators. This is what I have decided for my project to be as not only is it useful but also less costly than typical workforce. Furthermore, according to R&D, UVC Robots improve sanitization by 60% compared to liquid-based cleaning. The robot moves automatically in a defined area, after being programmed for things like time and distance from surfaces. It is made up of multiple lamps placed on top of a platform. During disinfection, the Robots emit UV-C irradiation at a wavelength of around 200-300 nanometers, allowing for a full 360-degree coverage. During the disinfection process, the robot moves at a steady speed, achieving a coverage of areas at several meters. However, to enable auto moving, the robot must be programmed via the use of a detailed map/blueprint of the hospital or medical clinic.  

This project to us is very useful as first, it would give me some experience of the key skills required for my dream job. Not only this but as stated above it would also fill the desires of my mum as i technically am working in the hospital. The main motivation behind this however is the need to do something for people. This is a genuine and sad fact that people mostly go to hospital as a way of obtaining medicine or treatment for their illness. Healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) are complications of healthcare and linked with high morbidity and mortality. Each year, about 1 in 25 U.S. hospital patients is diagnosed with at least one infection related to hospital care alone. 1 in 25!!! Can you believe this? For those who even go to a hospital or even a medical clinic for just a regular checkup could come back contracted with the worst of bacteria/infections. Some of the most common infections people transmit across medical clinics include but are not limited to: UTI, pneumonia and even lung infections. The need to help stop this due to lack of staff or lack of care provided, I feel that UV-C robots are the perfect way to transform our hospitals in the future if we genuinely want to save lives. 

Once every aspect is set, furniture and all other objects must remain in the same place to enable an autonomous functioning. Currently, according to my idea and for safety, this UV-C robot automatically shuts off when its motion sensor detects any moving things (whether it is a human or a vase falling) during the disinfection process. Despite the ultimate recommendations, manual cleaning in each hospital is based mostly on local protocol and cleaning staff’s private opinions, as well as the control and the inspection of their performance.  

Evidence further suggests that manual cleaning and disinfection are often inadequate and result in contamination (which we know to be true). Besides killing microorganisms on surfaces, disinfection robots automatically record the operation parameters of the disinfection process and by this, can provide efficiency and effectiveness assurance. Advantages of these robots also include but are not limited to disinfection will work in an unmanned manner, without the need for ongoing human presence at the disinfection site. Therefore, exposure of health care workers to harmful UV radiation can be avoided during the process. Applying UV-C as a final disinfection step after manual disinfection provides an additional hygiene benefit to reducing cross-transmission and associated infections which I mentioned above. UV light does not leave any residues, making this an environmentally friendly disinfection method.
